Fantasy music, at its heart, aims to create an immersive and evocative soundscape that complements fantastical themes, settings, and narratives. It's a genre that often draws inspiration from mythology, folklore, and fictional worlds, seeking to transport the listener to realms of magic, adventure, and wonder.

Key Characteristics of Fantasy Music:
✦ Instrumentation: Fantasy music frequently features a blend of orchestral instruments (strings, brass, woodwinds, percussion) to create a sense of grandeur and scale, particularly in epic or adventurous pieces. However, it also often incorporates instruments associated with specific cultures or historical periods that might fit a fantasy setting. Think of the use of:
✦ Celtic instruments: Harp, flute, tin whistle, bodhrán, often lending a mystical or folk-inspired feel.
World music instruments: Instruments from various cultures can be used to represent diverse fantasy races or lands, adding unique textures and colors.
✦ Electronic elements: In more modern fantasy scores, synthesizers and electronic textures can be used to create otherworldly or magical sounds.
✦ Mood and Atmosphere: The primary goal of fantasy music is often to create a strong sense of atmosphere. This can range from:
✦ Epic and heroic: Powerful brass and soaring strings often accompany scenes of battle or grand adventure.
✦ Mysterious and magical: Ethereal sounds, flowing melodies, and perhaps the use of a choir can evoke a sense of wonder and the arcane.
✦ Pastoral and idyllic: Gentle melodies played on flutes or acoustic guitars can paint a picture of peaceful, natural landscapes.
✦ Dark and foreboding: Dissonant harmonies, low drones, and dramatic percussion can create tension and a sense of danger.

Fantasy Music in Different Media:
✦ Film: Fantasy films are heavily reliant on music to enhance the emotional impact and immerse the audience in the world.
✦ Video Games: Music in fantasy video games plays a crucial role in setting the tone for different environments, from bustling towns to dark dungeons.
✦ Literature and Tabletop RPGs: While these don't have inherent soundtracks, many musicians are inspired by fantasy literature and create music that evokes the feeling of these worlds.
✦ Standalone Music: Many artists create music specifically within the fantasy genre, without being tied to a particular film or game.

Fantasy music is still popular nowadays. Nowadays it is used in most fantasy games.
